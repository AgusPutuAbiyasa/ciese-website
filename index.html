About CIESE

From Semiconductor Materials to Sustainable Energy Systems

CIESE is a multidisciplinary research center that connects fundamental semiconductor materials and device research with intelligent renewable energy systems. The center integrates computational materials, electronic devices, intelligent energy storage, IoT/SCADA, microgrids, and carbon valuation.

For 2026–2030, CIESE is positioned as a research, publication, prototype, policy-support, and grant-collaboration platform for fundamental research, applied research, regional policy grants, and international collaboration.

Vision

To become a leading research center in intelligent electronic systems and sustainable energy technologies that support low-carbon energy transition.

Mission

  • Advance research in semiconductor materials, electronic devices, energy storage, and smart energy systems.
  • Produce journal articles, datasets, prototypes, intellectual property, software, and policy briefs.
  • Build strategic partnerships with universities, ministries, BRIN, BRIDA, industry, utilities, and international institutions.

Research Clusters

Four Integrated Research Clusters

Each cluster has a dedicated grant pathway, output portfolio, and scientific role within the CIESE research umbrella.

▣

K1 — Computational Semiconductor Materials and Electronic Devices

Focus: ZnO, ZnO/perovskite interfaces, sensors, photovoltaic interfaces, and device physics.

Methods: parameter database, band alignment, drift-diffusion modeling, ML surrogate models, and PINN.

Outputs: computational articles, device datasets, teaching modules, and simulation toolkits.

Fundamental Grant
▰

K2 — Intelligent Energy Storage and Power Electronics

Focus: BMS, BESS, SOC/SOH, battery degradation, power converters, and storage diagnostics.

Methods: sensor monitoring, equivalent-circuit modeling, AI diagnostics, degradation modeling, and control.

Outputs: BMS prototypes, AI-BESS articles, battery datasets, and software/IP.

Applied Grant
◍

K3 — Smart Energy Electronic Systems

Focus: IoT, SCADA, EMS, smart monitoring, digital MRV, and energy-data infrastructure.

Methods: embedded systems, dashboards, time-series databases, forecasting, and anomaly detection.

Outputs: monitoring platforms, energy-carbon dashboards, EMS prototypes, and software/IP.

Applied / Innovation Grant
◒

K4 — Sustainable Renewable Energy and Carbon Valuation

Focus: FPV, PV–BESS microgrids, Nusa Penida, carbon valuation, MRV, and policy support.

Methods: techno-economic-carbon analysis, dispatch simulation, optimization, and policy mapping.

Outputs: policy briefs, roadmaps, Q1/Q2 articles, and MRV frameworks.

Policy / International Grant

Selected Publication Track Record

The CIESE research direction builds on a publication portfolio in nanoelectronics, semiconductor optoelectronic devices, smart electronic systems, and renewable energy. Detailed bibliographic records are available through the Google Scholar portfolio.

K1 Legacy

Semiconductor Materials and ZnO Optoelectronics

  • Ag-coated highly disordered ZnO films and ultraviolet lasing enhanced by surface-plasmon resonance.
  • High-temperature random lasing in ZnO nanoneedles.
  • Theoretical investigation of excitonic gain in ZnO–MgZnO strained quantum wells.
K1 Legacy

OLED, QD-LED, and Optoelectronic Devices

  • Charge transport, recombination, exciton harvesting, and device-performance studies in OLED/QD-LED related systems.
  • Optoelectronic device knowledge base supporting semiconductor-device physics and photovoltaic interface modeling.
K3 Applied Systems

Smart Electronic Systems and IoT Applications

  • IoT-based hybrid power and smart monitoring systems.
  • Smart water-metering, smart electrical-panel, furnace-control, and environmental monitoring applications.
K4 Renewable Energy

Floating PV and Renewable Energy Studies

  • Techno-economic and operational assessment of a 100 kWp floating photovoltaic system in Bali.
  • Renewable-energy system assessment for tropical, island, and land-constrained contexts.
K4 Policy Bridge

Energy Transition and Carbon-Valuation Pipeline

  • FPV, PV–BESS microgrid, Nusa Penida, diesel reduction, and carbon-economic valuation research pipeline.
  • Digital MRV and policy-support outputs for regional renewable-energy transition.
K2 Emerging

Intelligent Storage Research Pipeline

  • AI-based SOC/SOH estimation, BESS monitoring, and battery degradation-aware dispatch as the next applied research pathway.
  • Planned integration with PV–BESS, smart energy dashboards, and digital MRV.
